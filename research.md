## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **OMB 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance)** — federal framework governing administrative requirements, cost principles, and audit requirements for all federal grant recipients; significant updates effective FY2026 (single audit threshold raised to $1M, de minimis indirect rate raised to 15%)
- **Single Audit Act (2 CFR Part 200 Subpart F)** — requires independent audits for entities spending $1 million or more in federal awards annually (threshold raised from $750K in FY2026)
- **Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act (FFATA)** — mandates public reporting of federal awards; data published on USASpending.gov
- **XBRL / Data Act (2022)** — requires federal agencies to publish standardised, machine-readable grant data; compliance drives vendor reporting module requirements
- **SAM.gov registration** — mandatory registration system for organisations receiving federal funds; integration point for grant management systems
- **DUNS / UEI (Unique Entity Identifier)** — GSA-assigned identifier replacing DUNS for federal award recipients; required in system integrations

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The grant management software market was valued at approximately $3.22 billion in 2026 and is projected to reach $4.98 billion by 2030 at an 11.5% CAGR. Longer-range forecasts project growth to $8.09 billion by 2035.

**Funding:** Blackbaud is publicly traded. Euna Solutions and Fluxx have received private equity backing. The government grants segment received substantial tailwind from federal pandemic-era funding programs, sustaining demand through 2026.

**Pricing Landscape:** Small-to-midsize nonprofit tools start at $100–$500/month. Enterprise government-facing platforms (Euna, eCivis) run $50,000–$300,000/year. Foundation-facing platforms (Fluxx, Foundant) are priced on grant volume or application count. Cloud-based solutions now account for over 60% of market share.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Nonprofit grant writers and development staff seeking prospecting and compliance tools; community foundations managing grant cycles; government agencies administering federal and state grants; universities managing research grant compliance; corporate foundations running grant programs.

**Notable Trends:** FY2026 Uniform Guidance updates (raised thresholds, higher de minimis rates) are driving demand for compliance automation. AI-assisted proposal drafting is now a standard feature on leading platforms. Subrecipient monitoring and audit trail requirements are increasing complexity for government recipients. Federal DATA Act mandates are pushing vendors toward structured reporting.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- AI-assisted grant prospecting: ML models trained on grant databases (Grants.gov, foundation directories) could match an organisation's mission and programmatic focus to relevant funding opportunities with higher relevance scores than keyword search.
- Automated proposal drafting: LLMs fine-tuned on successful grant applications could generate first-draft narratives from project descriptions and budget data, significantly reducing staff writing time.
- Compliance monitoring and audit trail generation: AI agents could continuously monitor grant expenditure against budget categories, flag potential disallowed costs, and generate OMB 2 CFR Part 200-compliant documentation automatically.
- Subrecipient risk scoring: ML models could assess subrecipient financial health, past audit findings, and reporting reliability to generate risk-based monitoring plans, reducing manual oversight burden.
- Reporting and impact narrative generation: Generative AI could transform structured outcome data (participants served, milestones achieved) into grantmaker-ready narrative reports with appropriate framing and formatting.
